10-25 min

  • Go-to-market strategy optimization

25-45 min

  • Summarize key insights
  • Align on follow-ups
  • Timeline for next conversation

Healthcare Facility Client Session

Use this for existing or prospective healthcare facility customers. Focus: pain points, features, and optimization.

60-Minute Session Flow

0-10 min
Warm-Up & Current State
  • How's the platform working for you currently?
  • What's working really well?
  • What's frustrating or could be better?
10-30 min
Pain Point Deep Dive
  • Walk through their biggest operational challenges
  • Payment processing bottlenecks
  • Staff workflow issues
  • Patient experience gaps
30-50 min
Collaborative Ideation (Choose 2-3 exercises)
  • Feature prioritization exercise
  • Workflow mapping session
  • Dream feature brainstorm
50-60 min
Action Items & Timeline
  • Prioritize top 3 improvements
  • Set realistic timelines
  • Confirm next check-in

Brainstorm Prompts

üí° Current Workflow Analysis

Prompt: "Walk me through your payment collection process from patient arrival to funds in your account."

Follow-up: "Where do things typically slow down or break? What's the most manual part?"

üé® Dream Feature Exercise

Prompt: "If you had a magic wand and could add any feature to the platform, what would save your team the most time?"

Follow-up: "How many hours per week would that save? What would your team do with that time?"

üì± Patient Experience Focus

Prompt: "When patients use our system to pay, what do they say? What confuses them?"

Follow-up: "What would make patients more likely to pay upfront or use payment plans?"

üîÑ Integration Opportunities

Prompt: "What other systems do you use daily that should talk to our platform?"

Follow-up: "What's your biggest data entry headache right now?"

üí∞ Crypto Adoption Exploration

Prompt: "Have any patients asked about paying with crypto? Would you be interested in offering it?"

Follow-up: "What would make you feel comfortable accepting crypto payments?"

Feature Prioritization Exercise

Activity: Have them rank potential features using this framework:

  • Must Have - Can't operate effectively without it
  • High Value - Would significantly improve operations
  • Nice to Have - Would use it but not critical
  • Low Priority - Interesting but not needed now

Then discuss: What's blocking their #1 "Must Have" from being built?

üí° Pro Tip: Record (with permission) which specific features they mention most passionately. These are your highest-impact development targets and great testimonial opportunities.

Use this for potential integration partners, distribution partners, or strategic collaborators.

60-Minute Session Flow

0-15 min

  • Their current challenges/gaps

  • Co-selling opportunities
  • Referral relationship structure
  • Technology licensing

  • Use exercises from section below

  • Phase 1: Quick pilot/proof of concept
  • Success metrics for partnership
  • Resource commitments needed
  • Next steps and timeline

Brainstorm Prompts

ü§ù Value Alignment Exercise

Prompt: "What problem are your customers facing that our platform could help solve?"

Follow-up: "What would success look like if we partnered? How would we measure it?"

üîó Integration Mapping

Prompt: "Walk me through your current tech stack. Where does payment processing fit?"

Follow-up: "If we integrated with your platform, what data should flow between our systems?"

üéØ Go-to-Market Strategy

Prompt: "How do you currently sell to healthcare facilities? What's working?"

Follow-up: "Could we co-market to your customer base? What would that look like?"

üí° Quick Win Identification

Prompt: "What's the fastest, smallest thing we could build together to prove value?"

Follow-up: "Who would be a good beta customer from your network?"

üöÄ Scale Planning

Prompt: "If this partnership works, what could it look like in 12 months?"

Follow-up: "What resources would we each need to commit to get there?"

Partnership Framework Exercise

Activity: Map out potential partnership models together:

  1. Integration Partner - Technical API integration (what data flows where?)
  2. Referral Partner - Mutual customer referrals (what's the commission structure?)
  3. Co-Sell Partner - Joint go-to-market (how do we split deals?)
  4. Technology Partner - White-label or licensing (what's the pricing model?)

For each model, discuss:

  • What's required from each side?
  • What's the timeline to launch?
  • How do we share revenue/value?
üí° Pro Tip: Start with a small pilot project before committing to a large partnership. Define 30-day and 90-day milestones to ensure alignment and momentum.

Universal Brainstorm Exercises

üéØ The "What If" Game

How it works: Take any constraint and remove it.

  • "What if we had unlimited budget?"
  • "What if we could build this feature in 1 day?"
  • "What if we had 10x more customers tomorrow?"
  • "What if regulations didn't exist?"

Then ask: "What's the smallest piece of that we could actually do?"

‚ö° Lightning Round Ideas

How it works: Set a 5-minute timer. Each person shares as many ideas as possible, no filtering.

  • No idea is too wild
  • Build on others' ideas
  • Quantity over quality
  • Write everything down

After timer: Circle the 3 most exciting ideas to explore deeper.

üîç The Customer Journey Map

How it works: Walk through the end-user experience step by step.

  • What happens first? Then what?
  • Where do people get stuck or confused?
  • What makes them happy or frustrated?
  • What would make each step easier?

üé® Reverse Brainstorm

How it works: Instead of "How do we succeed?", ask "How could we completely fail?"

  • List all ways to guarantee failure
  • Then flip each one to identify what NOT to do
  • Reveals hidden risks and blind spots

üìä Impact vs Effort Matrix

How it works: Plot all ideas on a 2x2 grid:

  • High Impact, Low Effort = Do First
  • High Impact, High Effort = Strategic Projects
  • Low Impact, Low Effort = Quick Wins
  • Low Impact, High Effort = Avoid

Session Prep Checklist

  • Send calendar invite with clear agenda 48 hours in advance
  • Share any pre-read materials (deck, one-pager, demo link)
  • Prepare 5-7 questions tailored to this client type
  • Set up collaborative doc/whiteboard for capturing ideas
  • Test tech (Zoom, screen share, recording if permitted)
  • Block 15 minutes after session for immediate note cleanup

Post-Session Checklist

  • Send thank you + summary within 24 hours
  • Include: key insights, action items, next steps with dates
  • Assign owners to each action item internally
  • Add insights to your master feedback database
  • Schedule next touchpoint before momentum fades
  • Update CRM with conversation notes and tags
üí° Golden Rule: Listen 70%, talk 30%. The best insights come when clients feel heard and have space to think out loud. Your job is to guide, not dominate the conversation.
